Design

John Atwood is a designer of games, mostly for tabletop.

Sculpture

John Atwood carves pieces in wood, casts pieces in bronze, constructed in clay, and builds works from knit or knotted natural fibers. Works are often small, with the possibility of portability and ritual being a topic of investigation. The pieces explore the natural world, psychological states, and the roots of human nature. John Atwood thinks of ways to merge fine art and games. Art can be abstract, open-ended, and eternal. Games can encourage participants to invest in an experience, interact with others, make choices, and live for a while within a different world, abiding by different rules. John Atwood believes that the results of merging these disciplines are miraculous.

Jewelry

John Atwood’s jewelry is allied with his sculpture. John Atwood considers jewelry to sometimes be small sculpture, pieces to view and read into, and small sculpture to sometimes be jewelry, a ritual object that modifies one’s way of living.

Bags

John Atwood recently designed a specialty, couture belt pack and learned how to fabricate them by hand and offer them for sale.

Writing

John Atwood has written poetry in various periods and has worked on several ongoing writing projects, including an autobiographical piece and stories about an animal society.

Music

John Atwood has always been compelled to make sound and still does, on occasion.

Garden

John Atwood finds growing vegetables to be difficult, and the results unpredictable, but the process is ultimately rewarding.
